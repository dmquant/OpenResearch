UPS Q2 2025 Earnings Highlights

Financial Performance Overview

  • Consolidated Revenue: $21.2 billion (exceeded forecasts, but 2.7% decrease YoY)
  • Consolidated Operating Profit: $1.9 billion (non-GAAP adjusted)
  • Consolidated Operating Margin: 8.8% (non-GAAP adjusted)
  • Diluted Earnings Per Share (EPS): $1.55 (non-GAAP adjusted, slightly missed forecasts)
  • Free Cash Flow: $742 million
  • Dividends Paid Year-to-Date: $2.7 billion

Segment Performance

  • U.S. Domestic Package Revenue: $14.1 billion (0.8% decline YoY, average daily volume down 7.3%)
  • International Revenue: $4.5 billion (up 2.6%, driven by 3.9% growth in average daily volume)
  • Supply Chain Solutions Revenue: $2.7 billion (down 18.3%, due to divestiture of Coyote Logistics)

Key Strategic Initiatives

  • Targeting $3.5 billion in cost reductions for 2025 through network reconfiguration and efficiency initiatives.
  • Expanding healthcare logistics capabilities, with plans to acquire Andlauer Healthcare Group.
  • Continuing to reduce lower-margin business, particularly from Amazon.

Challenges Faced

  • Softer U.S. small package market.
  • Subdued manufacturing activity.
  • Impact of trade policy changes and tariffs.

Note: This visualization is based on summaries of the UPS Q2 2025 earnings call, as a full transcript was not publicly available.
